Vocal Range

What is a vocal range?

The broadest definition of "vocal range" is simply the span from the lowest to the highest note a particular voice can produce. In the context of singing, vocalists describe the vocal range as all the "musically useful" pitches that a person can sing. For example, a singer like Beyoncé has one of the most versatile vocal ranges, starting at a very low pitch of A2 and hitting a higher pitch of E6.

Note: When we write a pitch as A2, this is scientific pitch notation! The letter represents the pitch and the number represents the octave. The first 12 notes of a piano are the first octave, denoted as C1, D1, E1...

What are the types of vocal ranges?

Though everyone’s range is specific to their voice, most vocal ranges are categorized within 4 basic voice types:

  • Bass: the lowest male voice, E2 to E4.
  • Tenor: the highest male voice, B2 to A4, and possibly higher.
  • Alto: the lowest female voice, F3 to E5.
  • Soprano: the highest female voice, being able to sing C4 to C6, and possibly higher.

This is best to visualize on a piano!

What is my vocal range?

Knowing your vocal range will help you understand how high and low you can sing. It will also help you to learn where your weak spots are, and with advanced lessons, you will be able to expand your span of notes.
